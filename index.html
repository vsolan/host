"I believe that cinema is a show"

"I know I'm an outsider, an outsider, a director who makes weird movies that no one sees but everyone judges."

Jesús Franco Manera, also known as Jess Franco (Málaga, 12 May 1930 - Málaga, 2 April 2013), was a Spanish film director, actor, producer, scriptwriter, composer, editor and director of photography. He is considered one of the most prolific directors in the European exploitation genre and an outstanding figure, together with Paul Naschy, Narciso Ibáñez Serrador or Amando de Ossorio, of the Spanish horror films of the 1970s. His character of Doctor Orloff has been considered "one of the myths of the Iberian fantasy-terror". As a director he made around 200 films with a great deal of lesbian, erotic or pornographic sensibilities and the genres of fantasy, science fiction and horror films. His work as a director of vampire-themed films, with titles such as Count Dracula (1970), Vampires (1971), Killer Barbys (1996) or Killer Barbys vs.Dracula(2002) gained him great popularity.
